01
Introduction

Our brand is
our reputation.

For over 100 years, Scott Electric Group has earned the trust of South Texas. These guidelines ensure that every touchpoint — from a business card to a fleet vehicle — reflects the same standard of quality and professionalism that defines our work.

The Scott Electric Group brand is not just a logo or a set of colors. It is the sum total of every interaction we have with clients, partners, and our community. It represents 100 years of expertise, an unblemished safety record, and a deep commitment to the place we call home.

These guidelines exist to protect and project that identity. They are the single source of truth for how Scott Electric Group presents itself to the world. Anyone creating materials on behalf of Scott Electric Group — employees, vendors, agencies, or partners — must follow these standards.

Why brand consistency matters: A business that presents itself inconsistently appears smaller, less trustworthy, and harder to remember. For a company of our scale and history, inconsistency is a liability. These guidelines are a business asset as much as they are a design document.

This document covers

  • Logo usage, clearspace, and sub-brand system
  • Color palette with exact values for all formats
  • Typography hierarchy and font pairing rules
  • Photography and visual content standards
  • Brand voice, tone, and messaging framework
  • Application guidelines for print, digital, and fleet

Logo anatomy

SCOTT
ELECTRIC GROUP
Bolt mark — constructed from two intersecting parallelograms; represents electricity, energy, and forward momentum.
Wordmark — Playfair Display Bold, wide tracking, navy. Heritage serif that signals over a century of earned authority.
Division line — DM Sans Medium, ultra-wide tracking. Identifies the parent group, separates the display font tier.

Logo construction rules

  • The bolt mark is always gold (#D4A83A) on dark, and navy on gold backgrounds.

  • "ELECTRIC GROUP" is always in DM Sans 500, all-caps, letter-spacing 0.28em.
  • The mark and wordmark maintain a fixed proportional relationship. Never stretch, condense, or separately resize the elements.
  • Do not recreate the logo in PowerPoint, Word, or any non-vector tool. Always use the approved file assets.
  • The bolt mark is always vertically centered with the wordmark block, aligned to the center of the mark.

Logo Misuse

What never
to do.

These guidelines protect the integrity of the logo. Violating them dilutes the brand and makes Scott Electric Group appear unprofessional.

Never alter the logo proportions, colors, or typography. Never place it on conflicting backgrounds. The examples below are all violations.
Never do this
  • Stretch or condense the logo horizontally or vertically
  • Rotate or flip the logo or bolt mark
  • Change the bolt mark to any color other than gold, navy, or white
  • Place the standard logo on a busy photograph without a background treatment
  • Outline or add a stroke to the logo
  • Apply opacity, transparency, or blend modes to the logo
  • Use the logo on backgrounds that are close in value to the logo colors (navy on dark blue, gold on yellow)
  • Retype the logo in a different font
  • Separate the bolt mark from the wordmark except in approved standalone contexts
  • Add a drop shadow, glow, or any effect to the logo
  • Place unapproved graphics, badges, or copy inside the clearspace zone
  • Recreate the logo in Microsoft Office applications
These are the correct uses. When in doubt, refer to the approved asset library or contact the marketing team.
Always do this
  • Use approved SVG or PNG files from the brand asset library
  • Use the reversed logo (white + gold) on dark or navy backgrounds
  • Use the standard logo (navy + gold) on white or off-white backgrounds
  • Maintain minimum clearspace on all sides at all times
  • Scale the logo proportionally from a corner handle only
  • Use the gold lockup version on solid gold backgrounds only
  • When printing on colored stock, test proofs against brand color standards
  • Use the icon mark alone only when the full brand is already established in context
  • Contact [email protected] for any non-standard use case

Color usage proportions

The 70/20/10 rule: Navy should comprise approximately 70% of any branded composition. White and off-white neutrals make up 20%. Signature Gold is the accent at 10% — its power comes from restraint. Division colors follow the same rule within their own materials and should never appear in Scott Electric Group-level communications.

Correct color application
  • Navy as primary background, white for body, gold for accent and CTAs
  • Gold used for bolt mark, buttons, dividers, and key data callouts
  • Off-white used for page and card backgrounds in light-mode layouts
  • Deep charcoal (#2C2C2A) for body text on light backgrounds — never pure black
  • Division colors used exclusively in division-specific materials
Incorrect color use
  • Using bright or neon colors not in this palette
  • Using pure white (#FFFFFF) as a page background — always use Warm Off-White
  • Using pure black (#000000) for text — always use Deep Charcoal
  • Using division accent colors in parent brand materials
  • Mixing more than two division accent colors in a single composition
  • Using gold as a background color except in the approved gold lockup context

Iconography

Clear, functional,
consistent.

Icons in Scott Electric Group materials are always simple, line-based, and drawn on a consistent 24×24px grid. They reinforce utility and professionalism — they never decorate.

Icon style rules

  • All icons are drawn on a 24×24px grid with 2px stroke, rounded line caps and joins.
  • Icon color is always navy (#0F2040) on light backgrounds, or white on dark.
  • Gold is used for icon backgrounds (in filled circle or rounded square containers) to highlight key services or actions.
  • Never use flat-fill cartoon-style icons or solid silhouette icons — the style is always line-based.
  • Use icons consistently: if a service is assigned an icon, always use that same icon for that service across all materials.
  • The approved icon library is based on Phosphor Icons (regular weight). Do not mix icon libraries.

Photography

Show real work.
Real people.

Photography for Scott Electric Group should feel honest, substantial, and grounded in South Texas. We don't use stock photos — we show our actual projects, our actual team, and our actual community.

Industrial work site — dramatic lighting, scale visible
Industrial / Commercial

Broad shots that show the scale of our work. Refineries, commercial builds, infrastructure. High contrast, confident composition. Show the full environment.

Crew at work — candid, skilled, focused
Team & People

Real employees doing real work. Natural light preferred. Faces visible. Show expertise and focus — never posed "thumbs up" shots. Safety gear always correct and visible.

Finished project — residential or commercial
Project Results

Clean, professional "after" shots. These prove the quality of our output. Good lighting, straight lines, real environments. For Coastal Kitchens: warm natural light, lifestyle context.

Photography tone: Every photo should feel like it was taken on a job we're proud of, by someone who cares about the work. Warm, golden-hour light wherever possible. High contrast, not washed out. South Texas sky and landscape as backdrop when outdoors. The photo should make you feel the weight and competence of the work.

Photography guidelines

Photography do's
  • Use only real Scott employees, projects, and job sites
  • Always show correct PPE — hard hats, safety vests, appropriate gear
  • Prefer natural light or dramatic industrial lighting over flat fluorescent
  • Show scale — include people in shots of large industrial projects
  • Edit with warm tones that complement the Navy + Gold palette
  • Include South Texas environment (sky, coastal landscape) in outdoor shots
  • Capture genuine moments of skilled work over posed setups
  • Shoot Coastal Kitchens in finished interior environments with lifestyle warmth
Photography don'ts
  • Never use stock photography or AI-generated images in marketing materials
  • Never show employees without proper PPE, even for casual shots
  • Avoid highly filtered or heavily post-processed images that look artificial
  • Avoid dark, underexposed, or blurry images regardless of subject
  • Never show competitor branding, competitor vehicles, or competitor personnel
  • Do not use photos that show safety violations or non-compliant work conditions
  • Avoid clichéd stock-photo poses (handshakes, pointing at screens, fake smiles)

Spacing & Layout

Structure communicates
confidence.

Generous, consistent spacing is a mark of quality. Our layouts are never cramped. White space is not empty — it signals that we are organized, deliberate, and in control.

Spacing scale

16px

24px
Card padding, grid gaps, component spacing
32px
Section internal gap, divider margins
48px
Section heading to content gap
80px
Page section padding (top + bottom)

Grid & layout principles

  • Content max-width: 1100px. Always centered on page.
  • Page horizontal padding: 80px on desktop, 24px on mobile.
  • Prefer a 12-column grid. Standard content spans 8–10 columns.
  • Service and feature grids: 2-column on desktop, 1-column on mobile.
  • Asymmetric layouts (e.g. 60/40 split) are preferred over rigid 50/50 grids — they feel more editorial and dynamic.
  • Section backgrounds alternate between navy, off-white, and white to create visual rhythm and section separation.
  • Never place more than 3 columns of cards on a single row in print materials.

White space is a design element, not a mistake. Dense, overcrowded layouts signal anxiety and lack of control — qualities no client wants in a contractor. If a layout feels crowded, remove content before reducing spacing.

Brand Voice

How we speak
matters as much as
what we do.

Our voice is the written expression of our values. It should feel like it comes from a company with 100 years of experience and zero need to prove itself.

Voice characteristics

Confident, not boastful
We state our capabilities as facts, not claims. "100 years of South Texas work" not "We're the best electricians in Texas." Our record speaks. We don't need to shout.
Plain, not simplistic
We speak in plain English to all audiences. We don't use unnecessary jargon, but we don't dumb things down either. Clients respect honesty and directness.
Warm, not casual
We care about our community and our clients. That warmth shows through in what we choose to talk about — not through exclamation points and emoji. Professional warmth.
Specific, not vague
When we make claims, we back them with data. "EMR of .46" not "excellent safety record." "$250K in giving" not "generous community support." Numbers tell the truth better than adjectives.

Voice in practice

CORRECT VOICE
Scott Electric has been the electrical contractor South Texas industry trusts since 1920. With an EMR of .46 and OSHA VPP Star certification at two Valero sites, our safety record is not a talking point — it's a business requirement we've met for over a century.
INCORRECT VOICE
We're SO excited to be South Texas' #1 electrical company!!! Our amazing team of dedicated professionals is ready to tackle ALL your electrical needs with INCREDIBLE attention to detail and UNBEATABLE customer service. Call us today!
CORRECT VOICE — COMMUNITY
Last year, Scott Group contributed over $250,000 to the United Way of the Coastal Bend and supported 14 community organizations — from the Boys & Girls Club to the Corpus Christi Food Bank. This is not charity. It's what a company that belongs to a community does.

Grammar & style rules

Punctuation & formatting
  • Use an em dash (—) not a double hyphen (--) for parenthetical asides
  • No exclamation points in professional communications, proposals, or web copy
  • Use the Oxford comma in all lists
  • Spell out numbers one through nine; use numerals for 10 and above
  • Write "$250,000" not "$250k" in formal materials
  • Ampersand (&) in sub-brand names only — spell out "and" in body copy
Naming & capitalization
  • The company is "Scott Electric Group" or "Scott Group" — never just "Scott's" or "Scott's Electric"
  • Divisions: "Scott Electric," "Scott A/C & Heating," "Scott Telecom," "Coastal Kitchens"
  • The first mention in any document uses the full division name; subsequent mentions may shorten
  • "South Texas" is always capitalized as a proper noun
  • "Coastal Bend" is always capitalized
  • Job titles are lowercase in running copy: "our safety director" not "our Safety Director"

Digital & Web

Our digital
presence.

All digital touchpoints — the website, email, social media, and digital ads — must adhere to the same standards as print. Digital is often the first contact a prospective client has with Scott Electric Group.

Website standards

  • All pages load Google Fonts: Playfair Display (700, 900) and DM Sans (300, 400, 500, 600)
  • Brand colors are implemented as CSS custom properties using the exact hex values from this document
  • All pages include a "Get a Free Estimate" CTA in the header navigation at all times
  • The phone number 361-884-6326 must be present and tappable (tel: link) on all pages on mobile
  • No personal email addresses are displayed publicly — all contact routes through the contact form
  • Footer copyright reads "© 2025 Scott Electric Group. All rights reserved." — updated annually
  • All images include descriptive alt text for accessibility
  • Core Web Vitals targets: LCP < 2.5s, CLS < 0.1, FID < 100ms

Social media guidelines

  • Profile photos: bolt mark on navy circle, minimum 400×400px
  • Cover images: project photography with logo overlay, navy/gold palette
  • Post copy follows brand voice — no excessive hashtags, exclamation points, or emoji
  • Project photos must show work we are proud to put our name on — never share in-progress photos that show safety violations
  • Community giving posts are encouraged and should be warm but never self-congratulatory
  • Respond to all reviews and direct messages within 24 hours on business days
  • Do not argue publicly with negative reviews — acknowledge and offer to resolve offline

Approved platforms: LinkedIn (primary for B2B), Facebook (community and residential), Instagram (project photography and Coastal Kitchens), Google Business Profile (reviews and map). No TikTok or Snapchat required unless strategy changes.

Field uniforms

Standard field kit
ShirtNavy work shirt or polo, embroidered bolt mark + "Scott Electric Group" left chest. Division name optional right chest.
Safety vestANSI Class 2 high-visibility orange or yellow. Left chest: embroidered bolt mark + name.
Hard hatNavy or white. Scott Electric Group decal on front. No personal stickers.
PantsNavy work trousers or jeans — clean and in good repair. No torn or frayed hems.
FootwearASTM F2413 compliant safety boots. Color: brown or black. No athletic shoes on site.

Office & management

  • Navy or white branded polo for office staff — embroidered logo, left chest
  • Management attending client meetings: business casual minimum. Branded polo or dress shirt with Scott lanyard or pin acceptable.
  • Trade show and event staff: navy branded polo, khaki trousers, name badge on navy lanyard with bolt mark
  • Embroidery: bolt mark in gold thread; wordmark in white thread on navy; navy thread on white
  • Branded merchandise (mugs, hats, notebooks) uses the icon mark only — not the full lockup — to avoid crowding on small items

Embroidery digitizing: A production-ready DST file for the bolt mark + wordmark is available from marketing. Always use this file — never re-digitize the logo, as it will alter the proportions and detail of the bolt mark.
